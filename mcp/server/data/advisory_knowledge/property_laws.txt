# Property Law - India

## Property Purchase and Sale

### Due Diligence Checklist
1. **Title Verification**
   - Verify clear and marketable title
   - Check for encumbrances, liens, or mortgages
   - Verify ownership through title deed
   - Check for any pending litigation

2. **Document Verification**
   - Sale Deed
   - Encumbrance Certificate (EC) for last 30 years
   - Property Tax Receipts
   - Approved Building Plan
   - Occupancy Certificate (OC)
   - Completion Certificate (CC)

3. **Legal Compliance**
   - Verify zoning regulations
   - Check building bylaws compliance
   - Verify FSI (Floor Space Index) utilization
   - Check for any violations or deviations

### Registration Process
- Stamp duty payment (varies by state: 5-7% of property value)
- Registration fees (typically 1% of property value)
- Documents required: Sale deed, ID proofs, PAN cards, photographs
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks for complete registration

### Key Legal Provisions
- **Transfer of Property Act, 1882**: Governs property transfers
- **Registration Act, 1908**: Mandates registration of property documents
- **Indian Stamp Act, 1899**: Governs stamp duty

## Property Tax

### Assessment and Payment
- Property tax is levied by municipal corporations
- Based on Annual Rental Value (ARV) or Capital Value
- Payment frequency: Annual, semi-annual, or quarterly
- Penalties for non-payment: Interest @ 2% per month

### Tax Exemptions
- Agricultural land
- Properties owned by charitable trusts
- Government properties
- Properties under construction

## Rental and Lease Agreements

### Essential Clauses
1. Rent amount and payment terms
2. Security deposit (typically 2-10 months' rent)
3. Lock-in period
4. Maintenance responsibilities
5. Termination clause
6. Notice period (typically 1-3 months)

### Rent Control Act
- Applicable in certain states
- Protects tenant rights
- Limits rent increases
- Governs eviction procedures

### Legal Requirements
- Rent agreement should be registered if term > 11 months
- Stamp duty applicable (varies by state)
- Notarization recommended

## Property Disputes

### Common Disputes
1. Boundary disputes
2. Encroachment issues
3. Title disputes
4. Partition suits
5. Adverse possession claims

### Resolution Mechanisms
- Negotiation and settlement
- Mediation
- Arbitration
- Civil court litigation
- Consumer forum (for builder disputes)

## Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA)

### Key Provisions
- Mandatory registration for projects > 500 sq.m or 8 apartments
- Protects homebuyer interests
- Mandates project completion timelines
- Provides for compensation for delays
- Establishes dispute resolution mechanism

### Homebuyer Rights
- Right to information
- Right to timely delivery
- Right to quality construction
- Right to compensation for defects
- Right to refund with interest

## Inheritance and Succession

### Hindu Succession Act, 1956
- Governs property inheritance for Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, Sikhs
- Equal rights for sons and daughters
- Class I heirs have priority

### Muslim Personal Law
- Governed by Sharia law
- Fixed shares for heirs
- Different rules for male and female heirs

### Will and Testament
- Must be in writing
- Signed by testator
- Witnessed by two persons
- Can be registered (recommended)
- Can be revoked or amended

## Foreign Investment in Property

### Restrictions
- Foreign nationals cannot purchase agricultural land
- Can purchase residential/commercial property
- NRIs have same rights as resident Indians
- OCIs can purchase residential/commercial property

### Compliance Requirements
- RBI approval for certain transactions
- FEMA compliance
- Tax implications (TDS, capital gains)

## Key Recommendations for Property Transactions

1. **Always conduct thorough due diligence**
2. **Verify all documents with sub-registrar office**
3. **Get legal opinion from property lawyer**
4. **Check for any pending litigation**
5. **Verify property boundaries physically**
6. **Ensure all taxes are paid up to date**
7. **Get title insurance (if available)**
8. **Register all property documents**
9. **Keep all original documents safe**
10. **Maintain proper records of all transactions**
